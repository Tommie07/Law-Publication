CHUKWUDIFU AKUNNE OPUTA, J.S.C.

FRIDAY, 10TH JANUARY, 1996

CRIMINAL LAW - Provocation - Applicable Principles.

JUDICIAL PRECEDENT - Privy Council - Director of - Effect on the Court of Appeal

Issue:

Was there sufficient evidence of provocation so as to reduce the offence of the Appellant from murder to manslaughter?

Facts:

The Appellant was a Secretary of Design Group Limited a firm of chattered Architects. His marriage with the deceased came to be a result of a pregnancy presented to him by the deceased. Their domestic matrimonial history was one of intermittent quarrels, accusations and counter-accusations of infidelity against the Appellant and insubordination against the deceased. The deceased has always accused the Appellant of showing interest in their maid, a girl of about 13 or 14 years of age. The Appellant had on the other hand accused the deceased of smoking cigarette which he disliked.
